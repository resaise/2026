Call for Papers

The ReSAISE 2026 workshop invites researchers and practitioners to submit original work on the reliability and security of AI for Software Engineering. For the full list of relevant topics, please visit the home page.

Submission Types and Format

We invite submissions in the following categories:

  • Research Papers (up to 8 pages, including references): original, high-quality research addressing theoretical, empirical, or methodological aspects at the intersection of AI and Software Engineering.
  • Short Papers (up to 4 pages, including references): preliminary results, focused case studies, or well-defined contributions that are smaller in scope but offer valuable insights.
  • Dataset and Tool Papers (up to 4 pages, including references): descriptions of datasets, benchmarks, or tools that enable reproducible research and innovation. Submissions should include sufficient detail for reuse, and links to public repositories are strongly encouraged.
  • Position Papers / New Ideas (up to 4 pages, including references): early-stage research, emerging challenges, or forward-looking ideas about reliable and secure AI for Software Engineering.

Important: Please clearly indicate the type of your submission on the first page of the manuscript.
